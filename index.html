Can't Buy Me Love

No-one would dispute that The Beatles have been influential. Heck, they’re pretty much the most influential artist of all time. But is there anything Wikipedia can tell us about just how popular they are?

by Mitchell Lisle

Introduction

This data was produced by looking at all the articles on Wikipedia that link back to The Beatles main wikipedia page. As you can imagine, the topics that are seemingly related are super varied. From x to y, and even z.

Now, obviously measuring influence is difficult. The data from Wikipedia is by no means an entirely comprehensive record of this. This is merely a fun project to see where and how they have been recorded as having been part of everyday culture in ways no one ever imagined. Artists that Reference The Beatles in their works

For this I looked at the performers that are associated with the articles to see who is most commonly associated with The Beatles. Besides The Beatles themselves, a surprising (at least for me) winner is an artist by the name of Al Di Meola who is a Jazz and world music artist. Al Di Meola appears to have been greatly influenced by The Beatles in his works, of which there are quite a few, and even recorded a tribute album. Most of the other artists are not surprising, with Bob Dylan, Elvis Presley, Bee Gees, The Rolling Stones, The Beach Boys and Oasis all referencing The Beatles in a number of their articles.
